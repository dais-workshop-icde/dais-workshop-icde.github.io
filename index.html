First Workshop on
Data-AI Systems (DAIS)
Co-located with ICDE 2025, Hong Kong, China
May 19, 2025 (Monday)

Overview

The effectiveness of Large Language Models (LLMs) in accomplishing various knowledge intensive tasks has led to the emergence of compound AI systems where agents, powered by LLMs, complete tasks by interacting with external data retrievers, tools, and APIs. This new paradigm introduces exciting interdisciplinary challenges and necessitates rethinking approaches toward knowledge discovery and information retrieval as well as architecture design and usability of data systems. To this end, the proposed workshop aims to explore innovative approaches towards building data-aware compound AI systems in the era of LLMs while balancing objectives such as cost, efficiency, robustness, and interpretability. We encourage participation from academic and industry researchers as well as practitioners in data management, AI, and systems community and aim to foster interdisciplinary collaborations. It will be a full-day workshop involving invited talks representative of the work done in these communities, research presentations, and a panel discussion exploring the design space of compound AI systems.

Evaluation Criteria and Reviewing Process
The workshop will follow an anonymous review process, submissions will be reviewed in a double-blind manner, assigning at least three reviewers to each paper. The meta-reviewer will coordinate the review process, resulting in either an acceptance or a rejection.
